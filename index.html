    Listing in batches

    How high-volume sellers move 50 items a day — and which part Bynari does for you.

    Listing one item at a time is slow. The fast way is to work in batches: group like items, build one template, then fill each listing with only what changes. Here's the method — and where Bynari ends and eBay begins.

    1

    Get your photos in

    Bynari

    Shoot with your phone, sync them to your computer (iCloud or a cable beats the eBay app), and bring the photos into your library. Your library replaces the spreadsheet sellers keep to track what's what and where it's stored.

    2

    Group like items

    Bynari

    Put similar items together — all the women's tops, all the watch tools — so you set the category once, not fifty times.

    3

    Build one template for the batch

    Bynari

    Make a single template for that category holding everything that's the same on every item — department, condition wording, description boilerplate, shipping — then spin off one slot per item in the batch.

    4

    Fill each item — only what changes

    Bynari

    For each item: drop its photos in (the overall shot first, the tag second), write the title to the formula — brand, type, who it's for, size, color, the details that matter — and let Bynari fill the item specifics. All of them, not just the easy ones. Then add the price.

    5

    Bank them — don't publish

    Bynari

    Save each as a draft and mark it done. Nothing goes live yet — you build the whole batch first, tracking what's finished as you go.

    6

    Send the batch to eBay

    In eBay

    When the batch is ready, take it into eBay's bulk editor to schedule the listings — staggered, about a minute apart, going live the next day — and apply your promotions and store categories. Bynari hands you the finished drafts and a suggested schedule; the publishing stays in your account, in your hands.

    Bynari never publishes or schedules on eBay. It builds and banks the batch; you press go.

    Start a batch

    Build one template's worth of drafts at once. Pick the template, set how many, and Bynari spawns a draft for each — ready to fill.

    One draft is created for each, pre-filled from the template. You fill in what's different on each, then bank them.
